    Intro To Software Engineering

    Professor:

    Professor Wahaishi

    Summary:

    This course served as the introductory framework for our Software Engineering curriculum, establishing the foundational principles that are expected from our education at the Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT). The focal point of this course entailed engaging in a semester-long group project, consisting of 4-5 team members, dedicated to developing an online web checkers game while closely mirroring the typical software project lifecycle observed in industry settings. Each participant was assigned key roles that mirror real-world scenarios, such as project owner, scrum master, and developer. The course also emphasized the importance of conducting daily stand-up meetings within our teams, structuring and executing sprints, crafting user stories, estimating story points, and effectively managing our project backlog. In conclusion, this course delivered valuable insights into the practical aspects of project development, mirroring the daily experiences of software engineers in industry.

    Java
    Software Development Lifecycle
    Team Collaboration

    Engineering of Web Based SW Systems

    Professor:

    Professor Rabb

    Summary:

    This course provided a comprehensive overview of the responsibilities and knowledge required to excel as a full-stack software engineer, encompassing proficiency in backend, frontend, and database development. Through the introduction to database systems, I gained my initial exposure to SQL, enabling me to perform tasks such as data insertion, deletion, and modification. These skills subsequently proved invaluable during my internship at General Motors, a year later. Additionally, the course exposed me to the utilization and creation of REST APIs, serving as a vital conduit between database information and frontend presentation. Over the duration of the course, I not only expanded upon my preexisting skills in Python and HTML but also acquired new competencies in technologies like React, PostgreSQL, and REST APIs.

    Engineering of Software Subsystems

    Professor:

    Professor Hawker

    Summary:

    In contrast to my prior experiences with software engineering courses at my university, this particular course stands out due to its predominant focus on the software design aspect, as opposed to the mere implementation of code. Throughout the course, we delved deeply into the realms of design and the utilization of the Unified Modeling Language (UML), with a particular emphasis on the preeminence of design before embarking on the implementation phase. The curriculum seamlessly integrated essential design principles, encompassing sequence diagrams, as well as design patterns like composite, mediator, and proxy, which are widely employed in industry for the purpose of structuring and architecting project systems. This course has significantly broadened my perspective on software engineering, highlighting the fact that it entails more than simply writing code and hoping for functional outcomes, but rather necessitates meticulous system design tailored to the specific requirements of the software.

    Skills Learned

    Back-End Development
    UML Design Language
    System Design Patterns
