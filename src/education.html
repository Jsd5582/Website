    • Intro To Software Engineering

    Professor:

    Summary:

    This course served as the introductory framework for our Software Engineering curriculum, establishing the foundational principles that are expected from our education at the Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT). The focal point of this course entailed engaging in a semester-long group project, consisting of 4-5 team members, dedicated to developing an online web checkers game while closely mirroring the typical software project lifecycle observed in industry settings. Each participant was assigned key roles that mirror real-world scenarios, such as project owner, scrum master, and developer. The course also emphasized the importance of conducting daily stand-up meetings within our teams, structuring and executing sprints, crafting user stories, estimating story points, and effectively managing our project backlog. In conclusion, this course delivered valuable insights into the practical aspects of project development, mirroring the daily experiences of software engineers in industry.
